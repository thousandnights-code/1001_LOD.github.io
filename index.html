One Thousand and One Linked Open Data

A LOD project

The idea of linking different data related to the context of Middle-Eastern culture, was inspired by the concept of "intercultural meeting". One of the symbols of intercultural meeting is indeed the Persian medieval collection "One Thousand and One Nights" (also known as "Arabian Nights"). It is one of the most famous Middle-eastern literary work in the world, that inspired paintings, symphonic operas, literary works and even boardgames and movies, both in Eastern and Wester cultures. With the translation of the Arabian Nights tales in French, the fashion of "easternizing" the West began. The aim of this project is to find and describe the main concepts and entities related to One Thousand and One Nights: this was possible through the Linked Open Data available on the web.

Inside the legend

One Thousand and One Nights (or "Arabian Nights") is a collection of Persian tales and legends. The manuscript is dated to the Middle Ages and the author (or authors) is unknown. The main character is the persian princess Sheherazade, who was bethroted to a cruel sultan, known for murdering every wife he had during his life. The sultan believed the women were untrustworthy and unfaithful. Eventually, Sheherazade was forced to marry the sultan, and, to save her own life, she came up with a clever plan: every night, she told the sultan different stories and legends, thus catching his attention. Thanks to Sheherazade's narrations, the sultan not only spares her life, but also falls in love with her. The tales she told him are in fact those encompassed in the collection of "Arabian Nights".

What is LOD? How did it help in builing the project?

LOD, or Linked Open Data is structured open data interlinked to other data. Internet is thought as a great global database, whose interlinked data are both human and machine readable (Semantic Web). The standards used to share, link, describe, retrieve and read data are web technologies such as RDF, HTTP, and URIs. As it was said above, the purpose of the project is in fact to describe and link the entities and concepts related to One Thousands and One Nights, with the help of Linked Open Data and of different web technologies.

The Items

In this section, ten items (such as music productions, tangible work of arts, manuscripts) related to Arabian Nights (and, more broadly, to Middle-Eastern culture) are presented and briefly described. They stand as existing examples of man-made productions displaying a process of intercultural meeting.

Metadata Analysis

Metadata is "data that describes other data". In this case metadata provides contextual information retrieval about the ten items selected. The items are fully described and aligned, thus creating dinamic links between entities. Data is described and classified following specific standards provided by authorities and institution (i.e. DublinCore (DC) for literary sources, ISBN code for books, and so on...)

E/R Model

The entity/relationship model describes interrelated things in a specific domain of knowledge. It is composed of entity types and specifies relationships that can exist between entities (existing things that can be either conceptual or physical).

Theoretical Model

The theoretical model is the abstract bridge between the E/R model and the Conceptual model. While the former represents the connection between real items and entities, the latter expresses these relationships in abstract and general terms.

Theoretical Model graph

The theoretical model graph is the visual representation of this process of abstraction and is useful to provide a conceptual model.

Conceptual Model

A place, a concept, a human being, an object can be linked to other entities and described. This can be done by answering to the fundamental questions "who/where/when and what". The ontologies are used to provide a formal definition of the categories, properties and relations between the concepts and entities encompassed in the domain.

Data description, RDF serialization and Knowledge Graph

In the sections "Data Description" and "RDF Serialization and Knowledge Graph", the RDF (resource description framework) web technology is used to describe some entities (resoruces) and their relations (properties). The serialized data are in both human and machine readble form, the format used is RDF/turtle. The knowledge graph is the graphical visualization showing interconnections between the subject and its multiple properties.
